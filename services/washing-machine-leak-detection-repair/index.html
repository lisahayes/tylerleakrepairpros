Washing Machine Leak Detection & Repair in Tyler, TX

The rubber supply hoses behind a washing machine are among the most likely things in a Tyler home to burst and flood a room. They sit under full water pressure every hour of every day, even when the machine is off, and they fail with no warning.

Hoses, drains, and the machine itself

A washing machine leaks in three areas. The supply hoses, hot and cold, run under constant pressure to the valves on the wall, and an old rubber hose can split or blow off its fitting and empty hundreds of gallons into the room. The drain hose and the standpipe can overflow or leak where they meet. And the machine itself can leak at the pump, the tub seal, or the door on a front loader.

The supply hose is the dangerous one, because it fails under pressure whether or not the machine is running. A hose that lets go while the house is empty is a leading cause of major water damage, and it does not give the slow warning a fixture leak does.

Finding the leak point

We run the machine through fill, wash, and drain while watching each stage. A leak on fill points at the supply hoses or their valves. A leak during the spin and drain points at the drain hose, the standpipe, or the pump. Water under the machine when it is idle points at the supply side still under pressure.

We also check the standpipe itself, since a partial clog backs the drain water up and over in a way that looks like a machine leak. In a pier-and-beam Tyler home the laundry may sit over a crawl space, which lets us inspect the connections from below.

Repairing and preventing the next one

Worn rubber supply hoses are replaced, ideally with braided stainless lines that resist bursting, and the wall valves are checked and replaced if they no longer shut off cleanly. A leaking drain hose or standpipe connection is reseated or replaced, and a clogged standpipe is cleared so it stops backing up.

Leaks inside the machine, at the pump or the tub seal, are repaired where the unit is worth it. We will tell you honestly when an older machine leaking from the tub is past the point of paying to fix.

Why a laundry leak floods so fast

Unlike most household leaks, a washing machine failure can dump water by the gallon in minutes. A burst supply hose under full pressure floods a laundry room and runs into adjoining rooms before anyone notices, and in a two-story home it pours through the ceiling below.

That speed is why the supply hoses deserve attention before they fail. Replacing a ten-year-old rubber hose is a small job; cleaning up after one bursts is not.

A simple habit that prevents the worst

The single best protection is to shut the supply valves when the washer will sit unused for a while, and to replace aging rubber hoses on a schedule rather than waiting for one to fail. We can replace the hoses and add a lever valve that makes shutting them off easy. Call (903) 651-5125 and we will set it up so a burst hose is one less worry in your Tyler home.

Questions Tyler homeowners ask

Why are the washing machine hoses such a big deal?

They sit under full water pressure constantly, even when the machine is off, and old rubber hoses burst without warning. A failed supply hose is a leading cause of serious home water damage.

Water shows under the machine only when it runs. Where is it from?

A leak during fill points at the supply hoses; during spin or drain it points at the drain hose, standpipe, or pump. We run a full cycle and watch each stage to pin it down.

Should I replace my rubber hoses?

It is worth it. Braided stainless hoses resist bursting far better than aging rubber, and replacing a ten-year-old hose is a small job compared with a flood.

My drain overflows into the room. Is the machine broken?

Often not. A partially clogged standpipe backs the drain water up and over, which looks like a machine leak. We clear and check the standpipe as part of the diagnosis.

A hose burst and it is flooding. What now?

Shut the supply valves on the wall behind the washer if you can reach them, then call (903) 651-5125. A burst laundry hose floods fast, so stopping the water comes first.
