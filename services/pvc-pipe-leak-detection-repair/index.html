PVC Pipe Leak Detection & Repair in Tyler, TX

PVC rarely fails in the middle of a straight run. It gives at the joints, where a solvent weld was rushed or a fitting was stressed, and on Tyler drain lines it cracks where the ground shifts beneath it. Finding a PVC leak means reading the joints first.

Where PVC carries water in a Tyler home

PVC and its cousins handle the drains in most homes and the supply in many newer builds across Brookhaven, Briarwood, and the newer Stonebridge phases. It does not corrode the way metal does, which is its great strength. Its weak points are the joints and the way it responds to ground movement.

That second part matters in Tyler. Drain lines run through the same expansive clay that moves the slabs, and a rigid PVC drain bedded in shifting soil can crack at a coupling or pull apart at a hub when the ground swells and settles through the seasons.

Locating a PVC leak

For a PVC supply line under pressure, we confirm the loss with a pressure test and trace it acoustically, the same as any pressurized line. PVC carries leak sound a little differently than copper, so we adjust the listening, but the principle holds: follow the loudest point to the joint that is failing.

For PVC drains, which hold no pressure, a camera does the work. We feed it the line and watch for cracked hubs, separated joints, and the low spots where a shifted line now holds water. A locator on the camera marks the exact spot from the surface so any access is precise.

Repairing PVC the right way

A cracked fitting or a failed solvent joint is cut out and replaced with a properly cleaned and welded connection, then tested. The repair is only as good as the joint, so the prep matters: a rushed weld is what failed the first time, and we are not interested in repeating it.

Where the ground movement that cracked the line will keep working on it, we address the bedding or reroute the vulnerable section so the next freeze-and-swell cycle does not reopen the same failure. We explain what the line needs and what it costs before starting.

What a leaking PVC drain does

A cracked drain leaks waste water into the soil under and around the home, drawing the same problems an old sewer line does: saturated ground, roots seeking the moisture, and eventually a backup at the lowest fixtures. Because it is hidden underground, it often runs unnoticed until a smell or a slow drain gives it away.

Caught on camera early, a single cracked joint is a small, targeted repair. Left alone, it becomes a saturated yard and a line that has to be reset to grade.

Signs of a PVC failure

A slow drain that returns after every cleaning, a sewer odor near a cleanout, a damp spot over a known drain run, or weak pressure on a newer PVC supply line all point to a joint giving way. Call (903) 651-5125 and we will put a camera or a pressure test on it.

Questions Tyler homeowners ask

Does PVC really leak? I thought it lasted forever.

The pipe itself is durable, but the joints are the weak point, and ground movement in Tyler clay can crack a drain line at a coupling. Most PVC leaks we find are at fittings, not in the middle of a run.

How do you find a leak in a drain line that has no pressure?

A camera. We feed it down the line and watch for cracked hubs and separated joints, then a locator marks the spot from the surface so the dig is small.

Can a cracked drain joint just be patched?

It is cut out and replaced with a properly welded connection, not patched over. A rushed joint is usually what failed, so we prep and weld it correctly to keep it from recurring.

Why did my newer home's pipe fail already?

Often the original solvent weld was rushed, or the line sits in shifting clay that stressed a fitting. Newer does not mean immune when the joint or the bedding is the issue.

Can roots get into a cracked PVC drain?

Yes. Once a joint cracks and leaks, roots follow the moisture in just as they do an old clay line. The camera shows whether roots have already found the break, so the repair addresses both the crack and what came in through it.
