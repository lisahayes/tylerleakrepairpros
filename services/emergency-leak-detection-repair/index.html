Emergency Leak Detection & Repair in Tyler, TX

An emergency leak does not wait for business hours. A burst pipe, a flooding room, a ceiling beginning to sag: the first minutes decide how much it costs, which is why the line is answered around the clock and the response is same-day.

First, stop the water

When a leak is actively flooding, the single most useful thing you can do before we arrive is shut off the water. For a burst fixture line, the shutoff valve at that fixture may do it. For a major break, the main shutoff, usually where the service line enters the house or at the meter, stops everything. Knowing where yours is, before you need it, saves a lot of damage.

If you are not sure where the main shutoff is, call (903) 651-5125 and we can talk you to it while help is on the way. Stopping the flow is the first priority; finding and fixing the leak is the next.

What counts as a leak emergency

Some leaks genuinely cannot wait. A pipe that burst and is flooding, water pouring through a ceiling, a supply line spraying inside a wall, a sewer backing up into the house, or a slab leak undermining the foundation fast: these do real damage by the hour. The longer the water runs, the larger the eventual repair and cleanup.

In Tyler, the rare hard freeze is a classic trigger, splitting pipes and hose bibs that then let go when things thaw. A burst on a cold morning is exactly the kind of call the 24-hour line exists for.

Fast response, then real detection

An emergency call gets a fast, same-day response, but speed does not mean guesswork. Once the water is stopped and the immediate flooding is controlled, we still locate the leak properly, with the same acoustic, thermal, and tracer-gas tools we use on any leak, so the repair is precise rather than a rushed hole in the wall.

The goal is to stop the damage now and fix the cause right, not to trade one mess for another. Stabilize first, then locate, then repair.

Why minutes matter with water

Water damage compounds fast. In the humid Tyler climate, soaked drywall and flooring start growing mold within a couple of days, and a flood that spreads into multiple rooms or down through a ceiling multiplies the cleanup with every hour. Acting in the first minutes, shutting the water and getting help moving, is what keeps a burst pipe from becoming a remodel.

That is the entire reason for a 24-hour line. The leak that happens at 2 a.m. is often the one where fast action saves the most.
