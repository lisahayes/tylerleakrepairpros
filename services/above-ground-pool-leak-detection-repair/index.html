Above Ground Pool Leak Detection & Repair in Tyler, TX

An above ground pool leaks differently than one set in the ground. The water sits inside a wall and a liner above the soil, so a leak escapes from a known set of points, a fitting, a seam, or the liner itself, rather than disappearing into buried plumbing.

Where above ground pools give up water

Because everything is above the soil, the suspects are few and reachable. The skimmer and return fittings pass through the wall, and their gaskets dry out and leak. The liner can tear or pull away from its bead at the top rail. The hose connections to the pump and filter weep at their clamps. And the wall seam on some models can open with age.

That short list is the advantage of an above ground pool. The water that vanishes is going out through one of those points, and most are visible once you know to look, which makes the leak faster to pin down than an inground line buried under a Tyler yard.

Finding the leak point

We start by watching the water level and ruling out plain evaporation, which moves quickly in a Tyler summer. A marked bucket floated in the pool gives us the true loss rate. From there the level itself is a clue: water that stops dropping at the skimmer line points at the skimmer fitting; a drop that continues below it points lower, at a return or the liner.

We dye test the suspected fittings underwater, watching the dye pull into a gap, and inspect the seams and the liner bead around the top. With everything above ground, the leak rarely stays hidden for long once we work the list.

Repairing an above ground pool

A leaking skimmer or return fitting is resealed with a fresh gasket or replaced, a clean and inexpensive fix. A weeping hose connection is a new clamp or fitting. A small liner tear can be patched with an underwater patch rated for the job, while a liner that has gone brittle and is failing in several places is better replaced than chased.

We tell you which situation yours is. An aging liner near the end is not worth a string of patches, and we will say so plainly rather than send you back to the same leak in a month.

Why it still matters above the soil

An above ground pool leak may not threaten a foundation, but it still wastes water, runs the autofill or the hose constantly, and saturates the ground under and around the pool. A pool sitting on softened, eroded soil can shift or list, which stresses the wall and the liner further and turns a small fitting leak into a structural worry.

Catching it early keeps the base level and the repair small. A fitting gasket is a quick fix; a pool that has settled because the ground washed out is a much bigger one.

Closing-season and freeze notes

East Texas hard freezes, rare but real, can crack an above ground pool's exposed plumbing and fittings over winter. A pool that held water in fall and is losing it in spring often has a freeze-cracked fitting or hose. If you are seeing that pattern, call (903) 651-5125 and we will check the fittings and the equipment first.

Questions Tyler homeowners ask

Is the leak in the liner or a fitting?

The water level tells us. A drop that stops at the skimmer points at the skimmer fitting; a level that keeps falling below it points at a return or the liner. We dye test to confirm.

Can a liner tear be patched, or do I need a new liner?

A small, isolated tear can be patched underwater. A liner that has gone brittle and is leaking in several places is better replaced than patched repeatedly, and we will be honest about which yours is.

How do I know it is a leak and not evaporation?

A bucket test settles it. We float a marked bucket and compare its loss to the pool's. If the pool drops faster, water is leaving through something other than the air.

Could a leak make my pool lean?

It can. Water escaping into the soil under the pool erodes and softens the base, and a pool on shifting ground can list. Stopping the leak early keeps the base level.

My pool leaked over winter. Why?

A hard freeze can crack exposed fittings and hoses on an above ground pool. A pool that lost water over winter usually has a freeze-damaged fitting. Call (903) 651-5125 and we will check them.
