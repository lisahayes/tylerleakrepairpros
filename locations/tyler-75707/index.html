Southeast Tyler's suburban-era slabs

The 75707 ZIP is the heart of Tyler's suburban expansion era, the 1970s through early 1990s when families poured into the south and southeast side on new slab construction with copper supply. That copper, now forty to fifty years in service, sits at the point where pinholes begin to appear as the norm rather than the exception.

Many of these homes also added pools in the decades since, so the backyard plumbing in the ZIP is an odd mix of original-build copper inside and retrofitted pool equipment outside.

Copper at the pinhole threshold

The defining leak pattern in 75707 is aging copper on a slab. The homes of the 1970s and 1980s carry the same chloramine-susceptible copper as elsewhere in Tyler, but the sheer concentration of that era in this ZIP makes pinhole leaks a common call. A green stain at a joint, a warm floor, or a bill that crept up are the signs we chase most often here.

The slab also moves with the same expansive clay, compounding the stress on lines that are already aging.

Sorting house copper from added pools

With original copper inside and retrofitted pool plumbing outside, a 75707 homeowner who notices water loss faces the same question: is it the house or the pool? We test them apart. A pressure test on the house lines and a pressure-and-dye test on the pool isolate the actual source before anyone digs.

We also check the pool equipment pad, where fittings and unions loosen over time, since a fitting leak on the pad is the most common and quickest pool leak to fix.

The repipe question in 75707

When a home in this ZIP has had two or three copper pinholes, the question of repiping in PEX comes up honestly. One isolated pinhole is a repair. A pattern across the home is when the math favors replacing the pipe rather than chasing each failure. We give you the count of failures, the pattern, and the cost of both options, so the decision is yours with the full picture.

Questions from this part of Tyler

My 75707 home is from the 1980s. Should I worry about copper pinholes?

The copper in homes of that era is now at the age where pinholes begin. A green stain at a joint, a warm spot, or a climbing bill are the signs to call about.

We added a pool years after the house was built. Is that pool plumbing reliable?

Retrofitted pool plumbing varies in age and quality. We inspect the lines, fittings, and equipment pad carefully when a 75707 pool loses water.

How do I know if the water loss is the pool or the house?

We test them separately. A pressure test on the house and a pressure-and-dye test on the pool each point to a different system.

When does a repair become a repipe?

When the copper has failed in several spots, the pattern usually costs more to chase than a PEX repipe. We give you both numbers honestly.
