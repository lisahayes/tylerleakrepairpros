Leak Detection & Repair in Briarwood, Tyler

Briarwood is a comfortable south Tyler subdivision of 1980s and 1990s homes, slab-built, with many owners having added a backyard pool over the years. That combination of aging copper and added pools defines the leaks we find here.

Suburban slabs with added pools

The Briarwood homes are 1980s and 1990s slab construction with copper supply lines of that era. Over time, many owners added pools and spas, which were not always part of the original build, so the pool plumbing here varies in age and quality. Both the aging copper and the retrofitted pools are leak sources.

It is a settled, family-oriented neighborhood, and the homes have been lived in long enough that original copper and added pool equipment are both reaching the age where things start to go.

The leaks Briarwood brings

Slab and copper-pinhole leaks lead in the houses, with pool and water-heater leaks close behind. A pool dropping faster than evaporation, a warm spot on the floor, a green stain at a copper joint, or a water heater weeping at the base are the everyday Briarwood calls.

With both house plumbing and a pool in play, telling which one is losing water is often the first task, and we test them apart rather than assume.

Sorting house from pool

When a Briarwood home loses water, we determine whether it is the pool or the plumbing first. A bucket test and pool pressure testing isolate the pool, while a meter check and house pressure test point at the domestic lines. From there we pinpoint the specific leak for a small repair.

For the aging copper, we also flag whether it is an isolated pinhole or a pattern that points toward a repipe.

Aging copper and aging pools together

What makes Briarwood distinct is having both the era's copper and added pools reaching their later years at once. A home might have a copper pinhole and a pool fitting leak in the same season, which is why we check the whole property rather than stopping at the first leak we find.

Catching both early, the green stain at a joint and the slow pool drop, keeps two small repairs from becoming two large ones.

Questions from this part of Tyler

My Briarwood pool and house both seem to lose water. Which is it?

We test them apart. A bucket and pressure test isolate the pool; a meter and pressure test point at the house lines. That tells us which system is actually leaking before we open anything.

Do 1980s and 1990s Briarwood homes get copper pinholes?

Many do as that copper ages under Tyler's treated water. A green stain at a joint or a damp spot is the early sign, and we check whether it is isolated or a pattern.

My pool was added later. Does that matter?

It can. Retrofitted pool plumbing varies in age and quality, so we check the pool lines, fittings, and equipment pad carefully when a Briarwood pool loses water.

Should I worry about a repipe?

Only if the copper has failed in several spots. One isolated pinhole is a repair. We give you the honest pattern and both numbers.

How old are the pools in Briarwood?
