The original Tyler, in one ZIP

The 75701 ZIP holds the historic heart of the city. Downtown's early-1900s commercial blocks, the cherished pre-war homes of the Azalea District and Bergfeld, and the older north-central residential streets all share this code. Pier-and-beam foundations with crawl spaces are far more common here than in any other ZIP, and galvanized supply and cast-iron drains are the original materials.

That age is the defining leak factor. When we receive a call from 75701, we expect old pipe and we read the specific home's era before anything else, because a 1910 commercial building and a 1948 bungalow leak in different ways.

What leaks here

Crawl-space and sewer leaks lead in the historic residences, as galvanized supply corrodes and cast-iron and clay-tile drains crack at the joints. The mature trees of Bergfeld and the Azalea District send roots into any gap. Commercial buildings downtown add restroom-cluster and supply-run leaks in finished spaces.

A musty crawl space, a stained ceiling in a historic home, a slow drain that comes back, or a sewer odor near an old cleanout are the everyday 75701 calls.

Finding leaks in the oldest housing

In 75701 we lean on non-invasive tools that protect what is irreplaceable. Camera inspection reads old drains before anyone digs near a brick walk or a hundred-year-old tree. Acoustic and thermal tools find supply leaks in the crawl space or a wall without opening the historic fabric. Where a sewer needs repair, trenchless methods spare the azalea gardens and brick streets.

Protecting property while finding the source

Historic homes in 75701 carry finishes, floors, and gardens that cannot simply be replaced. A careless repair here costs far more than the plumbing it fixes. Every method we use, listening, imaging, camera, trenchless, is chosen to leave the home and its surroundings the way we found them.
