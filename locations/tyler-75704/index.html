Larger lots, looser soil

The 75704 ZIP covers the west and northwest parts of Tyler, where the soil shifts from the heavy black clay of the east to sandier, looser loam on many lots. Homes here tend toward larger parcels with more yard and more buried plumbing, and the sandier soil lets a pressurized leak travel a good distance before it shows at the surface.

That wandering is the defining west-side challenge. A wet patch on a large 75704 lot may be twenty feet from the actual break, which is why we locate by sound rather than by sight.

The leaks this soil produces

Underground, yard-line, and service-line leaks are the 75704 signature, fed by the sandy soil that lets them roam. Irrigation systems on larger lots add buried lines that leak into the same permeable ground. Supply and slab leaks appear in the various eras of housing here. A climbing bill with no visible source, a soggy patch in a dry stretch, or weak pressure across the house all point to the buried lines in this ZIP.

Acoustic locating on a big lot

Finding a buried leak in 75704 means correlating the sound rather than walking the wet spot. We charge the line and place acoustic sensors at accessible points, letting the correlator calculate where along the run the break sits. The result is one targeted dig on a large lot instead of a trench across the whole yard.

For an unpressurized drain or sewer line, a camera and a locator mark the spot from the surface so the repair is equally precise.

Mixed eras, unified soil

The 75704 ZIP spans enough eras of housing that the pipe material and foundation type change from block to block. What stays constant is the permeable soil that hides underground leaks. Whether the house is old or new, a buried leak here moves water before it surfaces, and locating it accurately is the whole job.

Questions from this part of Tyler

Why does my West Tyler (75704) wet spot move around?

Sandier west-side soil lets pressurized water travel underground before it surfaces, so the wet patch is often feet from the actual break. We locate by acoustic sound, not by digging at the wet area.

My bill climbed but there is no wet spot. Where is the leak?

Likely underground in the permeable soil, where it is soaking in rather than surfacing. We confirm the loss at the meter and trace the buried line acoustically.

Can you find a leak on a large 75704 lot?

Yes. A correlator calculates the break's position along the line between two sensor points, so a large lot does not mean guessing.

Could it be my irrigation?

Yes, especially on larger lots with extensive irrigation. We isolate the system by zone to tell it apart from a house line.

How do I reach you in 75704?

Call (903) 651-5125 any hour with what you are seeing or smelling, and we will head out.

Do your acoustic tools work on sandy soil as well as clay?

Yes. The correlator calculates the break by timing the sound between two sensor points, so the soil type affects how the water travels, not whether we can find the leak. Sandy soil just means the wet spot misleads more, which is exactly why we rely on sound rather than sight.
