Leak Detection & Repair in Hollytree, Tyler, TX

Hollytree is one of Tyler's premier neighborhoods, a gated golf community of larger homes built mostly from the 1980s onward. Big slab houses, established landscaping, and a pool on a good share of the lots shape exactly the kind of leaks we see here.

Larger homes, pools, and copper

The Hollytree homes are substantial slab-on-grade builds of the 1980s and 1990s, which means copper supply lines now reaching the age where pinholes appear, set on the same expansive clay that moves slabs across Tyler. Add the pools and spas common on these lots, and the leak profile is distinct from a smaller, older home.

Irrigation systems keeping the established landscaping green add another set of buried lines that can leak. A Hollytree property simply has more places water can go wrong.

The leaks we see in Hollytree

Pool and spa leaks are common here, along with slab and copper-pinhole leaks in the 1980s and 1990s homes. Foundation leaks follow as the clay moves the larger slabs. Irrigation leaks round it out. A pool that needs constant topping off, a warm floor, or a green stain at a copper joint are the everyday Hollytree calls.

With pools, irrigation, and aging copper all in play, finding which system is actually leaking is half the job, and we test them apart rather than guessing.

Detection on a premium property

We bring the full toolkit to a Hollytree home. Pressure and dye testing separate a pool leak from a plumbing leak. Acoustic and thermal tools pinpoint a slab or copper leak for a small, clean repair. Helium finds the tiny pool-line seeps that sound cannot catch. The aim is precise, low-disruption work on a finished, valuable home.

On a larger property with a pool and extensive landscaping, locating the exact source first is what keeps a repair from turning into a project.

Spas, water features, and the equipment pad

Beyond the main pool, many Hollytree properties have an attached spa, a water feature, or an elaborate equipment pad, and each adds fittings and lines that can leak. A spa heat-cycles hard and loosens its unions; a water feature has its own plumbing; the pad is a cluster of valves and connections that weep. When a Hollytree pool seems to lose water, we check the pad and the spa before assuming a buried line, because the easiest leaks to fix are often right there at the equipment. Isolating each system tells us exactly where the water is going.

Questions from this part of Tyler

My Hollytree pool keeps losing water. Can you find the leak?

Yes. We rule out evaporation with a bucket test, pressure test the plumbing lines, and dye test the shell to find whether it is a line, a fitting, or the shell itself.

Do the 1980s and 1990s Hollytree homes have copper pinhole problems?

Many do. Copper of that era is now in the window where pinholes appear, helped along by Tyler's chloramine-treated water. A green stain or a damp spot is the early sign.

Could my leak be the irrigation and not the house?

It can. Hollytree lots often have extensive irrigation, and a stuck valve or cracked line leaks into the ground. We isolate the system to tell it apart from a house leak.

Will the work disrupt my finished home and landscaping?

We pinpoint first precisely so the repair opens the smallest possible area, and trenchless options protect established landscaping where a buried line is involved.
