Leak Detection & Repair in Tyler, TX 75706

ZIP 75706 stretches along the north and northeast fringe of Tyler into older suburban territory, a quieter part of the city with a mix of longer-settled neighborhoods and rural transition. The homes here lean toward mid-century and older, and their service lines lean galvanized.

North fringe, aging infrastructure

The 75706 ZIP sits outside the concentrated growth that built up the core and the south side. Its homes are more spread out, many from the mid-century era or earlier, with the galvanized supply lines and older drains of that period. Some properties have been in the same family for generations, which often means original plumbing that has never been updated.

That untouched original infrastructure is the defining factor. The galvanized service lines here are among the oldest in use in the city, corroding internally and narrowing until flow drops or a pinhole finally breaks through.

The leaks of the north fringe

Service-line and supply leaks from aging galvanized lead the 75706 list. Old drains crack and slow. Where the properties edge into more rural character, longer supply runs through heavy clay add underground breaks that surface far from their origin. A whole-house pressure drop, a soggy strip near the meter, or a drain that returns after every cleaning are the everyday north-fringe calls.

Confirming and replacing a galvanized service line

We confirm a service-line leak at the meter, trace it acoustically to the break, then advise on the right repair. For old galvanized that has corroded end to end, replacing the whole run with copper or PEX is the honest call rather than patching one spot only to dig again next season.

Where the line is long and the lot is rural, trenchless replacement spares the property from a full open trench.

Quiet properties, quiet leaks

A characteristic of the 75706 area is that homes and their owners are often quieter, with longer time between visits to the property or slower notice of a climbing bill. A galvanized service line that has leaked into the yard for a season before anyone checks the meter is a common story here.

If a 75706 property has had weak pressure for a while, or a bill that crept up without a clear reason, the galvanized line is the first place we look. Call (903) 651-5125.

Questions from this part of Tyler

My north Tyler (75706) home has always had weak pressure. Is it the pipe?

Likely. Old galvanized supply lines corrode from the inside over decades, narrowing until pressure drops across the whole house. The service line from the meter is the first thing we check.

Is the galvanized pipe repairable or does it need replacing?

Corroded galvanized that has narrowed end to end is best replaced rather than patched at one spot. We give you both options honestly.

Can you replace it without a big trench?

Often, yes. Trenchless replacement pulls a new line along the old path through small pits, which is especially useful on the larger lots in this ZIP.

How do I know if the service line is mine or the city's?

From the meter to your house, the line is yours. We confirm the loss at the meter and trace the private side from there.
