Tyler's most established premium ZIP

The 75703 ZIP concentrates Tyler's upscale south-side communities: the gated golf neighborhoods, the master-planned developments, and the newer subdivisions pushing the city limits south. Homes here range from 1980s slab with copper to 2000s-and-newer construction with PEX, nearly all on the expansive clay that underlies the whole city.

The typical 75703 home has a pool, irrigation for established landscaping, and enough finished space that a leak matters both financially and aesthetically. There is more plumbing on a premium lot, which means more places water can escape.

The leaks that define this ZIP

Pool and spa leaks, slab leaks, irrigation failures, and copper-pinhole leaks in the older 1980s and 1990s homes make up the bulk of 75703 calls. The newer PEX homes lean toward fitting and slab issues rather than corroded pipe. A pool that drops faster than evaporation, a wet fairway lawn, or a warm floor are the everyday calls.

Sorting which system is leaking, pool, house, or irrigation, is always the first task on a premium south-side property, and we test them apart rather than guess.

Detection on a premium property

The full toolkit comes to a 75703 call. Pool pressure and dye testing, acoustic and thermal on the slab, irrigation zone isolation, and helium or tracer gas for the tiny pool-line seeps. The aim is precision so the repair opens the minimum on a finished, valuable property.

We schedule work to minimize disruption, and trenchless options protect the established landscaping these neighborhoods are known for.

Why even the newest 75703 homes leak

The expansive clay beneath the ZIP does not care how new the slab is. A PEX fitting in a 2010s home can fail just as a copper joint in a 1990s one can, and the pool and irrigation plumbing on any lot add more places water can go. Newer construction changes the kind of leak in 75703, not whether one happens.

We read each home on its own terms, starting with which systems are in play and which are most likely to be the source.

Questions from this part of Tyler

I live in Hollytree (75703) and my pool keeps losing water. Can you find it?

Yes. We bucket-test to rule out evaporation, pressure test the lines, and dye test the shell to find exactly where it is losing water.

How do you sort a pool leak from a house leak on my property?

We test them separately. Pressure and dye testing on the pool, meter and pressure testing on the house, and irrigation zone isolation each point to a different system.

My 75703 home is newer with PEX. Can it still get a slab leak?

Yes. The clay moves a new slab the same as an old one, and PEX fittings can fail. Newer changes the kind of leak, not whether one happens.

Will you protect my landscaping?

That is part of the job. We pinpoint first so the repair opens the minimum, and trenchless options spare established beds and turf.
